Let's start by breaking down the competency of Strategic Planning, as it relates to Agile coaching. Strategic Planning involves creating a long-term plan for an organization to achieve specific goals and objectives. In the context of Agile coaching, this means developing a strategic plan that takes into consideration the unique needs, goals, and challenges of an Agile team or organization.

To be effective in this competency, an Agile coach must possess a range of skills and practices, as well as a strong understanding of key areas of knowledge. Some key skills and practices include:

1. Leadership and management skills, including the ability to inspire and motivate a team, delegate responsibilities, and create a collaborative work environment.

2. Strategic thinking skills, which enable an Agile coach to assess an organization's strengths and weaknesses, identify opportunities and threats, and develop a clear and focused strategic plan.

3. Facilitation skills, which enable an Agile coach to lead meetings, workshops, and other collaborative sessions effectively.

4. Communication skills, including the ability to listen actively, articulate ideas clearly, and provide constructive feedback to team members.

5. Change management skills, which enable an Agile coach to help organizations navigate through transitions and adapt to new ways of working.

In terms of areas of knowledge, an Agile coach must have a deep understanding of Agile methodologies and practices, as well as broader knowledge of organizational behavior, project management, and business strategy. Some key reference materials to support this knowledge include:

1. Agile Manifesto - https://agilemanifesto.org/
The Agile Manifesto provides the foundation for Agile methodologies, and outlines the guiding values and principles that Agile coaches should follow.

2. The Scrum Guide - https://www.scrum.org/resources/scrum-guide
The Scrum Guide provides a detailed framework for implementing Agile principles and practices within Scrum teams.

3. The Lean Startup - https://theleanstartup.com/
The Lean Startup provides a framework for creating a culture of innovation and experimentation within organizations, which is a key part of Agile coaching.

4. The Art of Possibility - https://www.amazon.com/Art-Possibility-Transforming-Professional-Personal/dp/0142001104
The Art of Possibility provides powerful insights into leadership, creativity, and communication, which are all essential skills for an Agile coach.

5. Harvard Business Review - https://hbr.org/
The Harvard Business Review provides a wide range of articles and research on business strategy, organizational behavior, and other related topics that can help Agile coaches develop a comprehensive understanding of the organizations they are working with.

By developing a strong understanding of these skills, practices, and areas of knowledge, an Agile coach can be well-equipped to lead strategic planning efforts within Agile teams and organizations.
