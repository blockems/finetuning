Debate on Skills, Practices, and Areas of Knowledge needed to be at the level of Competent for the Competency Training and Development for an Agile Program Lead

Agile Program Lead is a key role in successful agile delivery. They are responsible for overall program-level planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing. To become a competent Agile Program Lead, certain skills, practices, and areas of knowledge are required. In this debate, we will discuss those skills, practices, and areas of knowledge in detail, along with supporting references.

Skills:

1. Leadership: Agile Program Lead should have strong leadership skills, including the ability to motivate and inspire teams. They should be able to create a shared vision among team members and align their efforts towards it. They must also be able to communicate effectively with stakeholders, sponsors, and team members. According to (PMI, 2017), leadership is one of the essential skills an Agile Program Lead should have.

2. Strategic Planning: Agile Program Lead should be skilled in strategic planning. They should know how to plan and execute a program-level strategy that aligns with organizational goals. They should be able to identify program-level risks and opportunities and develop appropriate responses to them. According to (PMI, 2017), strategic planning is an essential skill for an Agile Program Lead.

3. Collaboration: Agile Program Lead should be skilled in collaboration. They should be able to work with cross-functional teams and build strong relationships with stakeholders, sponsors, and team members. They should be able to facilitate effective communication and collaboration among team members. According to (SAFe, 2020), collaboration is a key skill for an Agile Program Lead.

Practices:

1. Agile Methodologies: Agile Program Lead should have a deep understanding of agile methodologies, including Scrum, Kanban, and SAFe. They should be able to apply the principles and practices of agile at the program level. According to (SAFe, 2020), Agile Program Lead should be proficient in agile methodologies.

2. Program Increment Planning: Agile Program Lead should be skilled in program increment planning. They should be able to plan, execute, and coordinate program-level planning events, including Program Increment Planning (PI Planning). According to (SAFe, 2020), Program Increment Planning is a key practice for an Agile Program Lead.

3. Continuous Improvement: Agile Program Lead should be committed to continuous improvement. They should be able to identify areas for improvement and take appropriate actions to address them. They should also encourage continuous improvement among team members. According to (PMI, 2017), continuous improvement is one of the practices essential for an Agile Program Lead.

Areas of Knowledge:

1. Agile Principles: Agile Program Lead should have a strong understanding of Agile principles, including Agile Manifesto and Agile Values. They should be able to apply these principles at the program level. According to (SAFe, 2020), a deep understanding of Agile Principles is one of the areas of knowledge essential for an Agile Program Lead.

2. Program Management: Agile Program Lead should have a solid understanding of program management. They should be able to manage a program’s scope, schedule, budget, and resources effectively. They should also be able to identify program-level risks and take appropriate mitigation actions. According to (PMI, 2017), Program Management is one of the essential areas of knowledge for an Agile Program Lead.

3. Business Acumen: Agile Program Lead should have a good understanding of the organization’s business objectives and stakeholder’s needs. They should be able to align program-level goals and objectives with organizational goals. They should also be able to make decisions based on business needs. According to (SAFe, 2020), business acumen is one of the essential areas of knowledge for an Agile Program Lead.

In conclusion, becoming a competent Agile Program Lead requires a combination of skills, practices, and areas of knowledge. This debate has discussed the essential skills, practices, and areas of knowledge required to become a competent Agile Program Lead. The Agile Program Lead should develop these competencies to lead agile delivery at the program level successfully.
