==========

Agile program leads are expected to have a high level of competency in stakeholder management as it is important to establish and maintain effective communication with all stakeholders throughout the program. An expert level of competency in stakeholder management requires a combination of skills, practices, and areas of knowledge.

Skills:

1. Communication Skills: The expert program lead should possess excellent communication skills to ensure that stakeholders are informed, engaged, and involved in the program. The program lead must have the ability to communicate complex information to different audiences with different levels of technical expertise.

2. Negotiation Skills: Stakeholder management requires the ability to negotiate and persuade stakeholders to align their interests with the program goals. Expert level program leads must have strong negotiation skills to resolve conflicts and reach consensus on program objectives.

3. Relationship Building: Developing strong relationships with stakeholders is essential to ensure that the program meets their needs and expectations. The expert program leads should be skilled in building and maintaining effective relationships with stakeholders.

Practices:

1. Stakeholder Analysis: Stakeholder analysis is a critical practice to identify and prioritize stakeholders based on their level of influence, interest, and involvement in the program. This helps the program lead to develop a stakeholder engagement strategy and plan.

2. Engagement Planning: The expert program lead must develop a stakeholder engagement plan that outlines how the program will communicate, involve, and engage stakeholders throughout the program lifecycle. The plan should also include strategies for managing stakeholders' expectations and feedback.

3. Effective Communication: The program lead must use effective communication channels to ensure that stakeholders receive information in a timely and understandable manner. This includes regular status reporting, newsletters, program updates, and presentations.

Areas of Knowledge:

1. Stakeholder Management: Expert program leads should have a thorough understanding of stakeholder management principles, techniques, and methods. They should be knowledgeable about stakeholder identification, analysis, engagement, communication, and influence.

2. Business Domain Knowledge: The expert program lead should have a deep understanding of the business domain in which the program operates. This includes understanding the organization's strategy, goals, objectives, and challenges.

3. Program Management: An expert program lead must have a comprehensive understanding of program management principles and practices, including program planning, execution, monitoring, and control. They should also be familiar with agile program management frameworks such as SAFe, LeSS, and Scrum@Scale.
