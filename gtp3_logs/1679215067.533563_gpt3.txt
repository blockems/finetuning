To be at the level of Lead for the competency Leadership for an Agile Program Lead, one must possess a wide range of skills, practices, and knowledge areas. These include:

1. Emotional Intelligence: As a leader, it is essential to have a high level of emotional intelligence. This includes self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills.

2. Collaboration: Collaboration is a key attribute of Agile Program Leads. The ability to work well with others, regardless of their role or level of expertise, is essential. This requires active listening, effective communication, and conflict resolution skills.

3. Strategic Thinking: An Agile Program Lead must be able to think strategically and see the big picture. This includes setting goals and objectives, developing strategies to achieve them, and monitoring progress.

4. Agile Methodologies: Agile Program Leads must have a deep understanding of Agile methodologies, including Scrum, Kanban, and Lean. They should know how to apply these methodologies to achieve the desired outcomes.

5. Change Management: Agile Program Leads must be skilled in change management, including planning, implementation, and communication. They must be comfortable with ambiguity and navigate complex stakeholder relationships.

6. Coaching: Coaching is a critical leadership skill that Agile Program Leads must possess. This includes the ability to provide feedback, mentor individuals and teams, and foster a learning culture.

7. Financial Management: An Agile Program Lead must be skilled in financial management, including budgeting and forecasting, risk analysis, and resource allocation.

8. Business Acumen: Understanding the business and market context is important for an Agile Program Lead. They must be able to make data-driven decisions and have a keen sense of the competitive landscape.

9. Technical Knowledge: An Agile Program Lead should have a strong understanding of technical concepts and architectures, including cloud computing, automation, and data management.
