Agile project management requires a unique set of skills, practices, and areas of knowledge for strategic planning. The lead for strategic planning competency should have a thorough understanding of the agile methodology, as well as the nuances of the organization they are working in.

Skills:
1. Strategic thinking and decision-making skills: The lead for strategic planning competency must be able to think critically and make informed decisions that align with the project's objectives and goals.
2. Communication and collaboration skills: The lead must also have excellent communication and collaboration skills to ensure everyone on the team is on the same page.

Practices:
1. Agile planning and estimation: The lead must be able to conduct thorough agile planning and estimation to ensure that the project is headed in the right direction and stays on budget.
2. Risk management: The lead must have a solid understanding of agile risk management and be able to anticipate and manage risks before they occur.

Areas of knowledge:
1. Product vision: The lead must have a clear understanding of the product vision and be able to communicate it to the team to keep everyone focused on the same goals.
2. Agile methodology: The lead must have a deep understanding of the agile methodology, including principles, practices, and tools.
3. Industry knowledge: The lead must be familiar with the industry they’re operating in and the factors that may impact the project's success.
